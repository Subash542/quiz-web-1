About the JFT-Basic

Objective of the Test:
The Japan Foundation Test for Basic Japanese (JFT-Basic) seeks to measure the level of Japanese language proficiency needed by foreign nationals about to reside in Japan mainly for work, to communicate in everyday life situations.
Target of the Test:
It is used to assess whether they have the Japanese language proficiency to be able to engage in everyday conversation to a certain extent and handle daily life without difficulties. This test is for foreign nationals who are not native speakers of Japanese. In particular, it is aimed at foreign nationals about to reside in Japan mainly for work.
Testing Method:
This test is conducted through Computer-Based Testing (CBT).
Test Structure:
Questions are set and answered on computers at test centers in each country. In booths, test-takers answer on the computer screen based on questions displayed on the screen and audio played through the headphones.
